    • Named Entity Recognition

    Visit the project on GitHub: GitHub link


    Objective

    The primary objective of using the bert-base-cased model for Named Entity Recognition (NER) is to automatically identify and classify named entities within a text corpus. These entities can be people, organizations, locations, dates, monetary values, or other predefined categories depending on your specific needs.





    Problem Statement/Case Study

    Imagine you have a large collection of customer reviews for a retail company. You want to automatically identify the names of products mentioned in the reviews to understand customer sentiment and buying trends. Manually identifying all product names in the reviews would be time-consuming and error-prone.

    Solution

    Here's how you can use the bert-base-cased model for NER to solve this problem.
    1. Data Preparation

  • Annotate a sample of your customer reviews with the named entities you want to identify (e.g., product names).
  • Preprocess your text data by cleaning and tokenizing it for the model.

    • 2. Model Training
  • Fine-tune the bert-base-cased model on your annotated dataset. This involves adjusting the model's weights to specialize it for NER on your specific domain (customer reviews).

    • 3. Entity Recognition
  • Use the fine-tuned model to process your entire collection of customer reviews.
  • The model will identify and classify product names (and potentially other entities) within the reviews.

    • 4. Analysis and Insights
  • Analyze the extracted product names to understand customer preferences, identify popular items, and gain insights into product sentiment.


    • Industry safety Detection

    Visit the project on GitHub: GitHub link


    Objective

    The primary objective of using YOLOv7 for industry safety detection is to develop a real-time computer vision system that automatically detects and identifies safety violations in an industrial setting like Workers not wearing proper personal protective equipment (PPE) like helmets, goggles, gloves, footwear/boots or jacket .





    Problem Statement/Case Study

    In a large manufacturing plant, it can be challenging for safety inspectors to monitor every worker and work area constantly. Manual inspections are time-consuming and may miss potential safety hazards. This can lead to accidents and injuries, causing downtime, production losses, and legal liabilities for the company.

    Solution


    1. Data Collection and Annotation

  • Collect video footage or images from various areas of the manufacturing plant.
  • Annotate this data with bounding boxes around workers, PPE, and potential hazards. Each bounding box should be labeled with the corresponding class (e.g., "worker without helmet," "improper lifting technique").

    • 2. Model Training
  • Train a YOLOv7 model on the annotated dataset. YOLOv7 is well-suited for this task because it's a real-time object detection model that can efficiently identify multiple objects within an image or video frame.

    • 3. Deployment and Monitoring
  • Deploy the trained YOLOv7 model on edge devices or cloud servers for real-time video processing.
  • The model will continuously analyze live video feeds from cameras installed in the plant.

    • 4. Alerting and Response
  • When the model detects a safety violation, it can trigger an alert (e.g., visual notification, audio alarm) to warn workers and notify safety personnel.
  • Safety personnel can then investigate the situation and take appropriate corrective actions.

    Solution

    • 3. Evaluation and Improvement
  • Evaluate the model's performance on a separate validation dataset to assess itsgeneralizability and avoid overfitting.
